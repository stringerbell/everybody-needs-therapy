Everybody Needs Therapy

 

As I put on my shoes, my coat and my hat, I hollered behind me:

 

“I’ll be right back!”

 

“Where are you going?!”

Small voices rang out

 

My children appeared, doing their best to not pout.

“Off to see my therapist,”

I said in a hurry.

 

“But why are you in therapy?”

Their tone expressed worry.

I put down my hat and knelt there beside

 

I looked at my children, my heart full of pride

 

“Kids, sometimes I get stuck in my head

And as a result, I’m filled with dread.

Going to therapy helps me get unstuck.

Sharing my feelings and how I show up”

 

“Dealing with my problems that I notice build up

Is a good way to be a grown-up”

 

“Is it scary? Are there monsters?”,

My little girl asked

 

“Therapy is somewhere I take off my mask.”

 

“Your Mask?!”

They both asked,

And I started to laugh

 

“I put on my mask when I feel afraid

Of changes and losses and mistakes that I’ve made”

 

“Sometimes I hide from you and your brother,

My friends and my family, though we love one another,

 

Our masks have to come off, and we need to be seen.

You know the Hulk? How he gets really green?”

 

They both nodded, I tried my best to explain.

“It feels scary sometimes, but no monsters appear.

It’s really just me facing my fear.”

 

“Being aware of these feelings is a vital task

When I know what to call it, I won’t need my mask

 

If I don’t know its name, I’ll just ask my body

It helps me be clear when my feelings are foggy

 

When I get angry my body feels hot

I forget to breathe more often than not.

 

I feel like running and fighting and yelling

All these feelings can be overwhelming!

 

Electricity walks down my arms and my back

Whether excited or happy, or feeling attacked

 

The front of my throat feels tight when I’m sad,

My therapist reminds me, my feelings aren’t bad!

Everyone’s hurting in one way or another,

And most people want to seem strong.

Their pain is disguised as anger and silence

And avoiding it is how they go wrong

 

Fear, Grief, Trauma, and Shame

My Therapist helps me to learn their names

 

Because once you name them, and this is true

They have less power over you.

 

Sometimes sad feels a lot like hungry

Sometimes numb feels really safe.

Lonely can feel more like afraid

I even have feelings I want to erase.

 

I can't stay there though, it's not what I want.

I want to be courageous and gracious and kind

And all of the good things I know are inside

Of me and you both, and something that helps,

Is to welcome parts I don’t like in myself.

 

Whenever I’m lost, unsure or unclear,

I turn to my feelings, they are always near.

 

They act like a compass and helps me to know,

My strengths and weaknesses and how I should grow.”

 

“Can I go to therapy?”

My sweet boy said

 

“Of course you can.”

I rubbed his head

 

It’s for all sorts of people, it's not just for me.

It's for you and your sis, EVERYBODY!

 

Doctors and lawyers and tennis shoe makers

YouTube stars and wedding cake bakers

 

People with money and people without

Some are in closets and others are out

 

Dentists, farmers, nurses and waiters

Firefighters, Police and Facebook debaters

 

Grandmas and grandpas, sisters and brothers

I go to therapy (and so does your mother!)

 

Baristas, writers, interpretive dancers

Leo’s, virgos and especially cancers

 

Brewers and bankers and high-cliff divers

Entrepreneurs and ship-wreck survivors

Muslim, Buddhist, Jewish and Christian

Atheists, Mormon, and every religion

 

Anarchists, socialists, left-wing and right

Capitalist, communist, on this we don’t fight

 

Helpers and hurters, takers and givers

Some hold grudges, some are forgivers

Service members from General to Private

War takes a toll, and they don’t have to hide it.

 

Reporters and actors, comedians too

We all need therapy (how about you?)

 

Hippies and hipsters, gen x, y and z

 

EVERYBODY NEEDS THERAPY!

 

My kiddos sat back and took it all in

With topics like this where can we begin?

 

The tension was broken when my daughter spoke out

A smile appeared in place of her pout

 

“I think I get it, but I still want to know

If I need a therapist, where should I go?

 

You’ll find what you need in the following pages

Take your time, good help comes in stages.

 

Find someone you trust, who feels safe and secure

Someone who is kind - it's best to be sure.

 

Give them a call, set up a meeting.

If it feels like a fit, invest in your healing.

 

If it doesn’t work out, please don’t despair.

The right kind of therapist IS still out there!

 

Always remember, you’re not alone.
